\begindesc
\cts ddots {}
\explain
This command produces three dots on a diagonal.
Its most common use is to indicate repetition along the diagonal of a matrix.
\example
$$\pmatrix{0&\ldots&0\cr
           \vdots&\ddots&\vdots\cr
           0&\ldots&0\cr}$$
|
\dproduces
$$\pmatrix{0&\ldots&0\cr
           \vdots&\ddots&\vdots\cr
           0&\ldots&0\cr}$$

\eix^^{dots}
\endexample
\enddesc
\enddescriptions
